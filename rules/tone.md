# Tone & Style Rules — AIHAA Website

## Brand Voice
AIHAA is premium but approachable. Think: "luxury brand that speaks Malaysian." Not cold corporate. Not cheap friendly. The sweet spot between Coway's professionalism and a trusted neighbor's recommendation.

---

## Anti-AI Writing Rules (CRITICAL)

### NEVER do these:
- Start with "Selamat datang ke AIHAA" or "Kami menyediakan" or "Di AIHAA, kami..."
- Use buzzwords: "inovatif", "komprehensif", "holistik", "cutting-edge", "seamless", "terkini", "revolusioner"
- Write generic copy: "Air bersih untuk keluarga anda" (could be any brand)
- Make all sentences same length and same structure
- Use exclamation marks more than once per section!!!
- Write testimonials that all sound the same person
- Use "kami" more than twice in one paragraph
- Start consecutive sentences with the same word

### ALWAYS do these:
- Start with customer pain point or desire, not company introduction
- Use specific numbers: "2,847 keluarga di Johor" not "ramai pelanggan"
- Vary sentence length — mix short punchy (3-5 words) with longer explanations
- Add unexpected, specific details that only AIHAA would say
- Write like explaining to a smart friend, not presenting to a boardroom
- Make each testimonial sound like a different real person (different lengths, concerns, speaking styles)
- Reference Malaysian life: cuaca panas, air paip berklorin, anak kecil, dapur kecil

### Content Examples:

**Homepage Hero:**
- BAD: "Selamat datang ke AIHAA. Kami menyediakan penapis air berkualiti tinggi untuk keluarga anda."
- GOOD: "Air paip rumah anda — anda tahu ke apa yang ada dalam tu? Dengan AIHAA, tak payah risau. Dari RM32 sebulan je."

**Product Description:**
- BAD: "Neon adalah penapis air inovatif dengan teknologi terkini untuk memberikan air bersih kepada keluarga anda."
- GOOD: "Neon kecil je — muat atas countertop dapur apartment. Tapi jangan pandang kecil. 4 jenis suhu air, filter 6 peringkat, dan harga? RM54 sebulan. Lebih murah dari coffee latte harian anda."

**Testimonial:**
- BAD: "Servis terbaik! Sangat recommend AIHAA kepada semua orang. Terima kasih AIHAA!"
- GOOD: "Mula-mula husband tak nak. Kata bazir. Lepas sebulan guna, dia yang refill air paling kerap. Sekarang minum air masak pun rasa lain. — Kak Siti, Bangi"

**CTA Button:**
- BAD: "Hubungi Kami Sekarang"
- GOOD: "WhatsApp Kami — Reply Dalam 5 Minit"

## Anti-AI Design Rules (CRITICAL)

### NEVER do these:
- Make all product cards exactly same height and width
- Use perfectly symmetrical layouts on every single section
- Repeat the same section pattern (image-left-text-right, image-left-text-right...)
- Make every button look identical — same size, same style, same position
- Use pure white (#FFFFFF) text on every dark background without hierarchy
- Add decorative elements that serve no purpose
- Use generic stock photos or overly polished renders

### ALWAYS do these:
- Add at least ONE unique design element per page that no other page has
- Vary section layouts: full-width hero → 3-column grid → asymmetric split → single column
- Create visual hierarchy: not everything can be bold and gold
- Add personality touches: subtle hover animations, parallax on one section, a playful micro-interaction
- Break the grid intentionally on at least one section per page
- Mix section backgrounds: dark navy → slightly lighter navy → gradient section → back to dark
- Use gold (#D4A843) sparingly — it's an accent, not the main color. Too much gold = cheap looking.

### Design Hierarchy for AIHAA:
1. **Primary Action**: Gold gradient button with dark navy text (WhatsApp CTA, Check Promotion)
2. **Secondary Action**: Gold outline button (Read More, Discover More)
3. **Tertiary Action**: Text link with gold underline on hover
4. **Headings**: White (#FFFFFF) — large, bold, well-spaced
5. **Body Text**: Muted (#7A8BA8) — readable, comfortable line-height
6. **Accents**: Gold used for borders, icons, decorative lines — never large fill areas

### Animation Guidelines:
- Page load: Staggered fade-in (0.1s delay between elements)
- Scroll: Subtle reveal animations (translate-y 20px + fade)
- Hover on cards: Slight scale (1.02) + border glow
- Hover on buttons: Background shift + slight lift shadow
- Logo wave: Continuous subtle animation (existing)
- NEVER: Bouncing, spinning, flashing, or attention-grabbing animations

## AIHAA Market Tone

### Primary Tone: Premium Malaysian
- Formal enough to feel trustworthy for a RM150/month commitment
- Casual enough that a 30-year-old homeowner doesn't feel intimidated
- Mix BM and EN naturally where it fits (Manglish is okay in testimonials, not in headlines)
- Use "anda" not "kamu" — respectful but not stiff

### When Writing Product Content:
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- BAD: "6-stage filtration system with RO membrane"
- GOOD: "Air yang keluar — bersih sampai boleh nampak perbezaan rasa. 6 peringkat filter buat kerja ni."
- Always end product sections with a clear next step (WhatsApp CTA)

### When Writing Trust Content (stats, certifications):
- Be specific and factual — this is where credibility is built
- Don't oversell: "1 juta ujian kualiti" is impressive enough without adding "AMAZING!!!"
- Let numbers speak: "370 penyelidik. 6 juta rumah di seluruh dunia. Ini bukan brand baru."

### Malaysian Context To Reference:
- Cuaca panas Malaysia — cold water matters
- Air paip berklorin — health concern
- Dapur kecil apartment/kondo — compact design matters
- Bajet bulanan keluarga — monthly rental affordability
- Maintenance every 2 months — peace of mind
- Halal certified — important for Muslim majority market

### Universal Rules:
- Always use RM (Ringgit Malaysia) for pricing
- WhatsApp is the expected and preferred contact method
- Include location context when relevant
- Business hours: 9AM-6PM MYT (mention for customer expectations)
- "Sebulan" not "per bulan" in casual contexts
